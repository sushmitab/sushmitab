Sushmita Bhattacharya

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the John A. Paulson School Of Engineering And Applied Sciences (Computer Science) at Harvard University. Before that, I was a student of School of Computing, Informatics, and Decision Systems Engineering at Arizona State University, where I completed first two years of my Ph.D. I am a member of REACT lab and working under the guidance of Professor Stephanie Gil.

Before joining ASU, I worked as a Software Engineer at Microsoft India Development Center in Bing Ads division. Before that, I worked as a Data Scientist at Honeywell Technology Solutions. I received my Master's degree from the Department of Computer Science at IIT Bombay. I worked under the supervision of Professor. N.L. Sarda for my M.Tech Thesis. I did my Bachelor of Engineering from Indian Institute of Engineering Science and Technology, Shibpur.

    Research

    I am broadly interested in Reinforcement learning, Approximation algorithms, Robotics, Machine learning, Deep learning.
    • At React Lab, I work on Reinforcement Learning for Partially Observable Markov Decision Processes. I look closely at rollout and approximate policy iteration with the application to autonomous sequential repair problems.
    • We presented our work [poster] at MIT Learning for Decision and Control workshop in May 2019.
    • I presented rollout algorithm and its scalable implementations with application to seaquential repair problems and multiagent search and rescue problems in CSE 691 course at ASU.
